What is SOFSAT?

SOFSAT, or Setlike Operator-based Framework for Semantic Analysis of Text, works with ideas taken from set theory, which consists of operations on unordered collections of items. Such operators are intersection and difference, where intersection finds the similarities between two sets, and difference finds the unique elements in a set. SOFSAT is a novel framework, first proposed by Dr. Karmaker, which consits of TextIntersect, TextDifference, and TextUnion. These operators act like their mathematical counterparts, but are designed specifically for semantic analysis of natural language text. For full details, look at the paper here.

On this site, we demo the first (small-scale) working iteration of the SOFSAT framework. We focus on two set-like operators, TextIntersect and TextDifference. TextIntersect, when provided two documents, finds the common information present in both documents. On the other hand, an operation of LeftSide TextDifference RightSide would find the elements present in the LeftSide document that were not in the RightSide document.

Motivation

The figure above illustrates an example of TextIntersect and TextDifference in action.

Two separate articles from two very different publishers are written about the same topic. Our SOFSAT framework is able to find the similarities and differences between the two articles. This effectively shows the bias that each side has, as well as the core ideas of the story.

Reporting biases from the "Left" are represented by coloring the text blue, while reporting biases from the "Right" are represented by coloring the text red. Text that is core to the story, as determined by our TextIntersect operator, is colored green.

Data

Data for this project was collected from Allsides.com, a website that gathers political news articles from both the "Left" and the "Right." It also gets stories from less biased, "Center" sources, as well as writing a "Theme" that describes the common topic of the three articles. We use this data to experiment with and evauluate our model.

Although we focused on political news stories for this demo, the idea of these text operators could be used in many different fields. Real datasets are scarce in this field currently, hopefully encouraging the research community to contribute data in this direction. Our results show promising signs for future research. Our plan for future research is to integrate the current state of the art Neural Network model to see whether it improves the performance.
