On Saturday, Motherboard reported that an anti-diversity manifesto penned by James Damore, a software engineer at Google, had gone "internally viral" at the company--and then, after Gizmodo obtained and published the 10-page document, it went viral-viral.
Throughout the memo, Damore dismisses internal programs that are supposed to address race and gender disparities at Google.
That someone felt comfortable disseminating the document in the first place, however, says even more about the company Google currently is.
