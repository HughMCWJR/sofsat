About 700,000 young immigrants who were brought to the United States illegally as children are shielded from deportation under an Obama-era initiative known as the Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals, or DACA.
After telling lawmakers last month that he would sign whatever they sent him, Mr. Trump now insists that any proposal address what the White House is calling "four pillars": protection for DACA recipients; an end to what Mr. Trump calls "chain migration," in which legal immigrants can sponsor their family members; an end to the diversity visa lottery, which is aimed at bringing in immigrants from underrepresented countries; and full funding for the proposed border wall, estimated to cost $25 billion over 10 years.
