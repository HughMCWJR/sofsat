As National Football League owners and players' union representatives meet in New York today and tomorrow to discuss the players' recent demonstrations -- the kneeling, linking arms or raising fists during the national anthem -- they should know how the law views these protests.
During World War II, the Supreme Court struck down such a demand for a flag salute during the Pledge of Allegiance.
