A caustic presence in a chaotic West Wing, Mr. Bannon frequently clashed with other aides as they fought over trade, the war in Afghanistan, taxes, immigration and the role of government.
Mr. Bannon can still wield influence from outside the West Wing.
In an interview this week with The American Prospect, Mr. Bannon mocked his colleagues, including Gary D. Cohn, one of the president's chief economic advisers, saying they were "wetting themselves" out of a fear of radically changing trade policy.
