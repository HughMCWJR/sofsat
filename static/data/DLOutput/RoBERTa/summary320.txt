A plan to allow transgender recruits to join the United States military beginning on Saturday has been delayed for six months by Defense Secretary Jim Mattis, a Pentagon spokeswoman said on Friday.
Mattis will allow service leaders to "review their accession plans and provide input" as they consider what impact adding transgender recruits would have on "the readiness and lethality of our forces," the spokeswoman, Dana W. White, said in a statement.
