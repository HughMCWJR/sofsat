The RAISE Act would bring about sweeping change to American immigration policy.
It would slash in half the number of green cards -- some one million -- issued today, throw out the diversity lottery aimed at attracting immigrants underrepresented in the U.S., and cut down on the number of refugees the U.S. admits.
Soon after the press conference, the Anne Frank Center slammed Trump's RAISE Act in a fierce statement likening it to an "ethnic purity test that harkens [sic] to the darkest chapters of world history."
