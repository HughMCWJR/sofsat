Since 2000, the number of high-poverty neighborhoods in America has doubled, rising faster in the suburbs, like Ferguson, Mo., than in cities, said Amy Liu, the director of the Metropolitan Policy Program at the left-leaning Brookings Institution in Washington.
"There's a lot of anxiety now, because this election was about the heartland versus the coastal elites; we're going to need a HUD secretary who governs both," Ms. Liu said.
The American Enterprise Institute, a conservative research organization, cited housing assistance programs as among those "ripe for reform'' under Mr. Trump.
