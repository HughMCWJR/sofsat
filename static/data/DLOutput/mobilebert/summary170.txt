Hurricane Harvey Fears of chemical plant explosion as Harvey floods shut down oil refineries Hundreds feared cut off by flood waters as storm moved east Wednesday, making landfall in south-west Louisiana Houses are seen submerged in flood waters caused by Tropical Storm Harvey in Houston Wednesday.
Photograph: Adrees Latif/Reuters An industrial chemical manufacturer has warned that its plant outside Houston could explode as fears turned to potential fuel contamination and 10 oil refineries in the impact zone of tropical storm Harvey were shut down.
It is unclear how big an explosion might be, and officials have not disclosed the amount of chemicals on site.
